The **Polkadot Events Bounty program** is a strategic initiative designed to foster community engagement and promote the Polkadot ecosystem through organized events. This program, **supported by the Polkadot treasury** and governed by community votes via **OpenGov**, aims to incentivize the organization and participation in Polkadot-related events worldwide. By providing financial rewards for these activities, the Polkadot Events Bounty seeks to position the Polkadot network, its teams, and contributors at the forefront of impactful global events, thereby driving community growth and ecosystem development.

## Polkadot Events bounty Objectives and Structure
The primary objectives of the Polkadot Events Bounty are to:
1. **Promote Polkadot Technology:** Enhance the visibility and understanding of Polkadot technology through targeted events.
2. **Foster Community Growth:** Support community-building activities across multiple regions globally.
3. **Enable Event Organization:** Provide resources and incentives for organizing events, ensuring diverse and high-quality community-driven gatherings.
4. **Streamline Event Oversight:** Create a transparent, efficient system for managing and executing events, supported by a dedicated team and robust infrastructure.
5. **Support Event Software Development:** Fund the development of software tools to aid in event management and metrics tracking.

## Polkadot Events Bounty Application Process and Eligibility
To participate in the Polkadot Events Bounty, interested individuals or groups must submit an application detailing their proposed event. The application is reviewed by the Polkadot team, which evaluates its eligibility based on the outlined criteria. Approved events can then proceed, and participants can claim bounty rewards after providing proof of their involvement and the event’s successful completion.

**Applications should include:**
- A multi-signature account with a set identity.
- Contact details (email and Telegram).
- Metrics for attendees, leads, reach, and satisfaction surveys.
- Detailed team information covering all aspects of event organization.
- A comprehensive marketing campaign and links to event tickets.
- Budget breakdown of all costs.
- An agenda or milestone plan for the event.
- Invoices for all expenses post-event.

## Polkadot Events Bounty Types of Events and Funding
The Polkadot Events Bounty funds various event types, including:
1. **Meetups:** Regular, cost-effective community events aimed at promoting Polkadot technology. Budgets for meetups are capped at specific amounts for venue, travel, photo/video, and advertising expenses. Organizers receive tiered rewards based on attendance, ranging from $250 to $500 per event.
2. **Key Industry and Extraordinary Events:** Larger, impactful events that require detailed proposals and substantial budgets. These events are subject to specific rules and requirements, including marketing campaigns, metrics tracking, and post-event reporting.

## Polkadot Events Bounty Reporting and Accountability
Post-event, organizers must submit a detailed report, including:

- Link to the original proposal.
- All invoices and proof of expenses.
- Comparison of proposed vs. actual metrics.
- Event description and agenda.
- Learnings and feedback from attendees.
- Event footage and marketing content.

Failure to comply with reporting requirements can result in blacklisting from future funding and addition to a public “Blacklist.”

## Polkadot Events Bounty Governance and Oversight
The Polkadot Events Bounty is governed by a team of curators who review applications, provide feedback, and approve or reject proposals. The curators also ensure that funds are allocated appropriately and that events meet the required standards. Regular office hours are held to facilitate communication between curators and applicants, offering a platform for real-time feedback and support.

### Polkadot Events Bounty: Empowering the Web3 Community Through Innovative Events

The Polkadot Events Bounty is a dedicated initiative within the Polkadot ecosystem designed to foster community engagement and promote innovation through a wide array of events. Established and managed by a team of curators elected via OpenGov, this program provides funding and support for event organizers committed to advancing Polkadot’s mission and values.

---

#### Objectives of the Events Bounty
The primary goals of the Events Bounty are to:

1. **Position Polkadot at Key Events**: Showcase Polkadot’s unique technology and ecosystem capabilities at high-profile industry events worldwide.
2. **Foster Community Growth**: Build and nurture vibrant local communities around Polkadot technology.
3. **Support Regional Initiatives**: Enable regional event organizers to contribute meaningfully to Polkadot’s global strategy.
4. **Promote Transparency and Efficiency**: Ensure a fast and transparent process for event planning and funding.
5. **Drive Innovation in Event Management**: Fund and develop tools and technologies that improve event execution and metrics collection.

---

#### Who Can Apply?
The Events Bounty welcomes applications from:

- **Passionate Event Organizers**: Individuals or teams with a proven track record of successful event management.
- **Community Members**: Active participants in the Polkadot ecosystem eager to host meetups or series events.
- **Professional Organizers**: Experienced professionals with robust networks within the blockchain and Web3 communities.

---

#### Funding Opportunities
The Events Bounty offers flexible funding models tailored to different event categories:

1. **Key Industry Events**:
   - Funding: No budget cap; depends on the event’s scope and impact.
   - Proposal Requirements: Must be submitted at least four weeks before the event.
   - Examples: Conferences, workshops, and presentations aligned with Polkadot’s strategic goals.

2. **Extraordinary Events**:
   - Funding: No budget cap; evaluated based on innovation and engagement potential.
   - Proposal Requirements: Must be submitted at least four weeks before the event.
   - Examples: Hackathons, side events, and sponsorships outside standard categories.
   - Current Limitations: Proposals are only considered for Sub0 and Decoded events.

3. **Meetups and Series Events**:
   - Funding: Up to $2,500 per standalone meetup; $10,000 for series events.
   - Proposal Requirements: Must be submitted at least two weeks before the event.
   - Additional Rewards: Organizers can earn up to $500 based on attendee engagement and impact.

---

#### Application Process
The application process is designed to ensure alignment with Polkadot’s values and goals:

1. **Choose an Event Type**: Select the appropriate event category and download the relevant template from the Templates Folder.
2. **Submit Your Proposal**: Use the online submission form to provide detailed event information and supporting documents.
3. **Initial Review**: Curators evaluate proposals for alignment with strategic goals and eligibility requirements.
4. **Feedback and Refinement**: Applicants receive constructive feedback to refine their proposals.
5. **Approval and Funding**: Successful proposals are awarded funding on-chain.

---

#### Reporting Requirements
Transparency and accountability are core principles of the Events Bounty. Organizers must submit a detailed report within one month after their event, including:

- **Metrics**: Compare proposed objectives with actual results.
- **Financial Documentation**: Provide all invoices and proof of expenses.
- **Event Footage and Marketing Content**: Share photos, videos, and promotional materials.
- **Learnings and Impact**: Reflect on successes, challenges, and overall outcomes.

Failure to submit a complete report can result in blacklisting from future funding opportunities.

---

#### Special Initiatives: Polkadot Roots
Polkadot Roots is a new program under the Events Bounty designed to empower community leaders to organize impactful meetups in key Web3 hotspots. This initiative provides tools, resources, and guidance to build strong local communities aligned with Polkadot’s vision. Current pilot locations include Miami and Southern Nigeria, with plans for global expansion.

---

#### Exclusions and Limitations
The Events Bounty does not fund:

- Luxury accommodations and dining.
- Team-building activities or per diems.
- Agency or management fees.
- Unnecessary or excessive costs.

Proposals must adhere to strict transparency standards, and curators reserve the right to reject funding requests that do not align with these principles.

---

#### Conclusion
The Polkadot Events Bounty represents a significant investment in the growth and innovation of the Polkadot ecosystem. By supporting passionate organizers and fostering meaningful events, this program helps build a connected and engaged community while showcasing the transformative potential of Polkadot technology.
